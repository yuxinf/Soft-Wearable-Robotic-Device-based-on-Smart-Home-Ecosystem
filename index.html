Soft Wearable Robotic Device Based on Smart Home Ecosystem

Existing Soft Wearable Robotic Devices (Exoskeleton) mostly focus on structure design and force control. These devices work under certain circumstances and are unsuitable to take home for daily use. To extend the application fields, we take IoT technologies into consideration. Our project based on the Exoskeleton, which improves the mobility of patients with lower-limb paralysis. We designed and developed a module to help the Exoskeleton fuse into smart home ecosystem. Our module contains three major functions: computer vision system, inertial measurement sensors (IMU) system and patient-centered speech recognition control. Computer vision system detects the current environment to choose the most appropriate control policy. IMU system processes the data to calculate the knee angle and torque supplied for real-time monitoring. The patient-centered speech recognition control helps to tackle with emergency conditions and improve patients' emotional stability and social-integration.

Motivation

Motivation

In the last two decades, Exoskeletons have been heralded as one type of promising assistive device for performance augmentation of healthy individuals and medical rehabilitations of patients with disabilities, however most of these Exoskeletons are designed to be used in hospitals or rehabilitation centers. In order to truly improve the mobility of patients with lower limb paralysis in their daily lives, our team decided to modify an Exoskeleton prototype and develop a module to enable it to fuse into smart home ecosystem with advanced IoT technologies.

System

The system comprises three main elements: camera module, IMU system. and speech recognition control. For the camera module, it pipelines the photo to the google cloud server. Based on these photos, we apply a pre-trained neural network to help us classify different environmental conditions. The system will choose the most appropriate control policy based on that result. The second one is IMUs system. We put a IMU on our thigh and shank. Each IMU send the data to a microcontroller. The microcontroller will process these data to calculate the knee angle. Based on knee angle, we can find out the torque required exoskeleton to provided for patient. In additional, The doctor and patient family can monitor the data through the APP. The third function is the patient-centered speech recognition control. We interfaced with APIs to enable to patients to send tweets from our device via speech recognition. By sharing the real-time status on twitter, patients can inform their family, doctors, and friends in time in case any emergency happens.

Results

Our module could distinguish stair environment and non-stair environment successfully. We trained our model on the dataset (MCIndoor20000) with 16000 pictures and got the accuracy about 98% in test. The detection result could be reflected by the detection light (red for stairs, green for non-stairs) on app. Our app could also show the knee angle, torque and their variation in real-time for the essential monitoring. The doctor or family members could monitor the condition of exoskeleton and give the necessary help. The speech recognition system could send twitter about the emotion of patients and emergence call successfully. The goals from bronze to gold are all completed.
